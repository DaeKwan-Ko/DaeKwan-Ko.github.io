Dae Kwan Ko is an Assistant Professor - Fixed Term in the Plant Research Laboratories at Michigan State University 
where he investigates on how biological molecules interact each other, creating biological networks, in plants in
response to environmental stress. In his research, he plans to apply his multi-omics modeling approaches to understand 
plant-microbiome interactions for plant resilience against climate change. He received a PhD in Plant Biology from
the University of Texas at Austin in 2016 where he was advised by Z. Jeffrey Chen, and he received MS in Biological
Sciences from Seoul National University and BSc in Crop Science from Konkuk University.


where he was advised by Jitendra Malik, and he received a Honours BSc in
Computer Science from the University of Toronto in 2007. He received a National
Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellowship in 2009, the C.V. Ramamoorthy
Distinguished Research Award in 2013, the PAMI Young Researcher Award in 2020.
His works have received awards at ECCV 2016, TPAMI 2016, ECCV 2020, ICCV 2021, 
CVPR 2022, and the Communications of the ACM (2022).
